# Simple Kit Forms

This plugin allows for the quick creation of forms that can be added to pages and posts using the block editor. All responses are recorded in its own database and can be exported at any time.

## Highlights

- Simple form creation.
- Quick embedding into pages and posts.
- Review and manage responses directly on the dashboard.
- Export responses as spreadsheets.
- Spam protection using reCaptch or Akismet.

## How the plugin works

After activating the plugin, first access the "settings" page in the SK Forms menu of your dashboard. The first adjustment to make is to define the spam protection for your forms. The recommended method is reCaptcha. To do this, you need to provide the keys supplied by the service, which can be created for free on the service's website at [https://www.google.com/recaptcha/](https://www.google.com/recaptcha/). Another form of spam protection is the use of Akismet. You can also proceed without protection, but this is not recommended. Next, define whether or not you want to log the IP address of the visitor who responds to your forms.

<img width="1920" height="973" alt="forms01" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b86d10ce-5e95-4fde-8e23-8149c52f0098" />

Next, go to the "create form" page. Here, you must give your form a title, specify the thank-you message after it is completed, and enter the text for the submit button.

<img width="1920" height="973" alt="forms02" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a462bfe6-b021-4bac-95ee-0fe6bb3a2da4" />

After that, in "field palette", add as many fields as you want to your form and click "create form".

<img width="1920" height="973" alt="forms03" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/56c4fa79-57d6-45c2-bf49-a7f3f3182657" />

Your form will be available along with the others you create on the "forms" page of the menu. From this list, you can edit, check the responses received, or even remove the form.

<img width="1920" height="973" alt="forms04" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/2e3a3eff-8d63-446d-b9ea-1b6beaddacc5" />

When editing a page or post where you want to include the form, simply add a "Simple Form Kit" block and, in that block's settings, specify which of your forms should appear. You can also adjust the form's rendering using the custom CSS field.

<img width="1920" height="973" alt="forms05" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/58f827cd-88c1-4005-8032-4edfc4ce650b" />

When reviewing form responses, you'll immediately see the most recent ones in a list, allowing you to directly check details about each one. You can also remove them individually or in groups, and even export them all as a CSV spreadsheet.

<img width="1920" height="973" alt="forms06" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4e4c8760-7e35-45c6-b061-01f29e963fd0" />

Additionally, on the "backup" page you can make a backup copy of all your forms and received responses, which can even be used to import them into another WordPress installation.
